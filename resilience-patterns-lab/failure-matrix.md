# Failure Matrix

## Mini-lab 1: Timeouts

**Observable claim:** Without a client timeout, a hanging upstream consumes the
client's entire connection/concurrency budget and healthy requests queue
behind dead ones; with a timeout, throughput to healthy paths recovers.

| # | Scenario | Prediction (verbatim) | Observed | Verdict | Takeaway |
|---|----------|------------------------|----------|---------|----------|
| 1 | hangProbability=0.3, latencyMs=20, concurrency=10, timeout=0, rate=20/s, 20s | "1. yes success/sec drop to 0 near the end, 18sec mark 2. it does not e=recover on its own" | Collapse to success=0 by **t=3s** (not t=18s); stayed at 0 for remaining 17s, `fail`/`timedOut` also 0 throughout — `sent` climbed into the void | Recovery-claim ✓ / Timing ✗ (magnitude miss, ~6x) | A hang probability applies per *draw*, and sockets are reused repeatedly — expected draws-to-hang = 1/p, so all N sockets get consumed within a few socket lifetimes, not gradually over the run. ## Mini-lab 2: Retries with exponential backoff + jitter

**Observable claim:** Naive immediate retries against a degraded upstream multiply offered load and deepen the outage (retry storm); exponential backoff reduces it; jitter breaks the synchronized retry waves visible as pulses in the per-second counters.

Backoff fixes synchronized retry bursts, not a structural "average demand > capacity" deficit. |

(Parking lot: the classic "jitter smooths thundering herd" scenario needs a *synchronized* failure burst, e.g. all in-flight requests failing at once from a shared blip — this capacity-bound setup doesn't isolate that effect; would need a separate scenario with a scripted mid-run blip to see it cleanly.) |

Retries compound the per-attempt failure rate multiplicatively — the same compounding math that deepens a storm under capacity constraint (scenarios 1-3) is what makes retries powerful when there's headroom to absorb the extra attempts. |
